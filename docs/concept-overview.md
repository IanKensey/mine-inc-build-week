# Mine Inc. Concept Overview

## Project definition

Mine Inc. is a space-based industrial strategy game centred on survival, resource acquisition, manufacturing, and modular expansion. The Build Week project is a focused playable demonstration of that broader concept.

## Elevator pitch

Command a vulnerable escape pod in deep space, mine passing asteroids, recover their resources, and manufacture modules that turn a fragile refuge into the beginning of an industrial operation.

## Target player experience

The player should feel like the operator of a small but growing space enterprise. Progress comes from observing the environment, choosing useful targets, issuing deliberate commands, and seeing those decisions produce visible changes to the vessel and its capabilities.

The experience should be readable and purposeful rather than frantic. Early vulnerability should give way to a sense of control, productivity, and physical growth.

## Command-and-observe model

The player directs activity from an external 3D view instead of piloting every action directly. They inspect the surrounding space, select objects, issue commands, and observe equipment carry out those instructions. Clear trajectories, selection feedback, resource movement, and construction results make the state of the operation understandable.

## Immediate gameplay loop

1. Survey nearby space from the escape pod.
2. Identify an asteroid and observe its trajectory.
3. Select and mine the asteroid until it breaks apart.
4. Collect the released resources.
5. Use those resources to manufacture a module.
6. Place the module on the pod's 3D attachment grid.
7. Repeat the cycle with a more capable vessel.

## Longer-term game vision

Beyond the Build Week prototype, Mine Inc. could grow into a deeper game of industrial expansion in space. A larger experience may support increasingly capable vessels and stations, broader production chains, strategic choices, exploration, and escalating operational challenges.

These ideas describe the direction of the wider concept, not commitments for the Build Week scope.
